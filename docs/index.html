Which countries like the same music?

What Spotify's Top 200 song charts tell us about global music taste

By Amy O'Kruk

Published October, 2019

Music has never been more accessible. With the rise of music-streaming platforms, listeners can play the majority of songs ever made by anyone for free. Spotify — with 232 million monthly users — leads the pack.

And wherever you are, whatever you listen to, Spotifiy keeps track of it all. The music service publishes top song charts for 60-plus countries around the world everyday.

So how do these countries stack up against each other? Who likes what and which countries like the same songs? Let’s take a look.

From Peru to Panama, Latin America listens to the same tunes

Spotify publishes top 200 song charts for more than 60 countries worldwide. Countries are scored based on how many song choices they had in common.

Latin American countries have a strong, shared taste for genres like Reggaeton and trap

In Latin America, popular music is often dominated by what’s known as urban music: genres such as ▶ reggaeton, Latin hip hop, Latin trap and dembow.

Songs in this space have racked up billions of streams on Spotify. Many also pointed to a Latin music boom in the U.S. following Luis Fonsi’s 2017 hit ▶ Despacito

In June, many countries throughout Central and South America (plus Mexico and Spain) were listening to the same artists: ▶ Bad Bunny, a Puerto Rican Latin trap and reggaeton singer; ▶ Sebastian Yatra, known for Latin pop and reggaeton; and ▶ Paulo Londra, an Argentinian rapper and latin trap artist, among others.

Brazil’s top songs were almost completely different from those in the rest of Latin America.

Part of the reason is Brazil’s unique history. The former Portuguese colony is the lone speaker of the language in the region. Many of its popular genres are unique to the nation: bossa nova and samba, to name just a few.

▶ Sertanejo, a type of Brazilian pop music evolved out of local folk music, has been the most played music genre on Brazilian radio since the 1990s, constantly topping the Brazilian music charts.

In June, Brazilian sertanejo artist,▶ Marília Mendonça, known for Latin pop and reggaeton; and , dominated the country’s top song chart with 15 tracks. Songs from other Brazilian sertanejo artists such as Zé Neto & Cristiano and Gusttavo Lima also topped Brazil's chart, but nowhere else.

Data and methods:

To collect the data, I scraped Spotify’s Top 200 songs per country for the week of 06/21/2019 to 06/28/2019.

Spotify’s top song charts heavily favour countries from North America and Europe. While I included every available country, nearly all of Africa and many parts of Asia are not represented here.

To create the correlation matrix, I calculated the jaccard index of different top 200 song lists. I used a clustermap method to hierarchically order the data by similarity and then by continent.

One limitation of this analysis is that the data represents a single week, offering us a snapshot of trends. More concrete observations could be made if the data represented a larger time period.
